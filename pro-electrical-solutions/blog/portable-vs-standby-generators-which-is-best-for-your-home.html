What are Portable Generators?

Portable generators are small, self-contained units that can be moved from one location to another. They're often used for camping, tailgating, and other outdoor activities, but they can also be used as a backup power source for your home. Portable generators are typically less expensive than standby generators, and they're often easier to install. However, they do require more maintenance and can be noisier than standby generators. What are Standby Generators?

Standby generators, on the other hand, are permanent installations that are connected to your home's electrical panel. They're designed to automatically turn on when the power goes out, providing a seamless transition to backup power. Standby generators are more expensive than portable generators, but they're also more powerful and reliable. They require less maintenance than portable generators and can be fueled by natural gas or propane. For homes in Daphne and Fairhope, standby generators are a popular choice due to their reliability and convenience.

Key Differences Between Portable and Standby Generators

Here are some key differences between portable and standby generators to consider:

  • Power output: Standby generators can produce more power than portable generators, making them a better choice for larger homes or homes with high power demands.
  • Automatic startup: Standby generators can automatically turn on when the power goes out, while portable generators require manual startup.
  • Installation: Standby generators require professional installation, while portable generators can be installed by the homeowner.
  • Maintenance: Standby generators require less maintenance than portable generators, but they do require regular maintenance to ensure they're running properly. Our electrical repair services can help with any maintenance or repair needs.

Which Type of Generator is Best for Your Home?

The type of generator that's best for your home depends on your specific needs and circumstances. If you live in an area with frequent power outages, a standby generator may be a better choice. However, if you're looking for a more affordable option and don't mind manual startup, a portable generator may be the way to go.
